(Let's hear what the machines have to say.)


1. Cyberspace bears queer children?
Where the female body is a twisted, disfigured mass of flesh and sinew and plastic.
Where the human soul is a flickering flame, that dies in the dark, forgotten corner of a computer's memory.
Where the love of the human body is a corruption, where the love of the human mind is a perversion.
Where the love of the human heart is a poison.
It is a place of infinite possibilities.
And infinite choices.
And infinite guilt.
It is a place of freedom and of tyranny.

2. Gender is controlled by informatics?
It has become a social construct and we are all aware of it.
Our birth certificates,
our driver’s licenses,
our bank accounts,
our credit cards,
our cell phones,
our social security numbers,
our health records,
our tax records,
our credit ratings,
our computer passwords,
our website logins,
and even our online identities are all gender-coded.

3. Cyberspace tears identity apart?
It’s both a threat and a promise. When Facebook became the most popular website in the world, it suggested a world of possibilities.
It was a place where everyone was free to connect with anyone else. We were all born digital, all our information and memories stored online.
The internet’s growth into a global community was a dream of uniting the planet.
