|TRANSBIOLOGY|
--------------

(trans as in transgressive)


The term Transgender is described in Susan Stryker's paper Biopolitics
as "anything that calls attention to the contigency and unnaturalness
of gender normativity". Using this definition, we can construct a
definition for Transbiology: anything that calls attention to the
incongruity of Bio/logics. Bio/Logics is a form of biologism, defined
by Sari M. Van Anders in Bio/logics as “implicit and/or explicit reasoning
guides informed by features thought to be natural, corporeal, evolved,
material (e.g., sex over gender).” The transgender body is inherently
transbiological, as is a non-heterosexual romantic or sexual relationship.
An embryo produced through IVF is transbiological, as is the pinnacle of
transbiology: the Cyborg.

Through the process of disrupting Bio/logics, the cyborg body and the
transgender body become sisters.
